## Overview
[cite_start]This repository contains the framework proposing an alternative analog for market impact: dislocation dynamics in quasicrystals[cite: 4, 5]. [cite_start]Standard models of market impact assume that resistance to order flow is local and mean-reverting, analogous to dislocation motion in a periodic crystal (Peierls-Nabarro pinning)[cite: 4]. 

[cite_start]This framework demonstrates that in quasicrystals, dislocations carry a six-dimensional Burgers vector whose perpendicular (phason) component leaves a non-canceling damage trail—a **phason wall**—that increases retarding stress proportionally to path length[cite: 5, 6].

## Core Concepts
We map this structural behavior directly to financial markets:
* [cite_start]**The Physical Component:** The observable price change corresponds to the physical Burgers component[cite: 7].
* [cite_start]**The Phason Component:** The invisible restructuring of volatility surfaces, correlation structures, bid-ask spreads, and dealer positioning corresponds to the phason component[cite: 7, 8].

[cite_start]Market impact accumulates as a phason wall, producing self-locking liquidity crises that standard historical covariance-based risk models (VaR, expected shortfall) fail to anticipate[cite: 9, 12]. 

[cite_start]Furthermore, central bank liquidity interventions (such as quantitative easing and repo facilities) are reinterpreted within this framework as **thermal phason healing**[cite: 10].
